Network Analysis of EV Charging Infrastructure

Optimizing Route Planning and Accessibility in California

Overview

As electric vehicle adoption grows, efficient charging infrastructure is critical to minimize range anxiety and support sustainable transportation. This project applies network analysis to evaluate spatial accessibility and propose optimizations across Californiaâ€™s charging network, with a focus on San Francisco.

Methodology

  • Collected EV station data using Open Charge Map API and routing info from Mapbox.
  • Constructed a spatial graph where nodes are stations and edges are geodesic distances.
  • Applied betweenness and degree centrality to detect key hubs and poorly connected areas.
  • Used Voronoi and buffer analysis to identify charging deserts and underserved zones.
  • Visualized routes and clusters to suggest optimal station placements and accessibility gaps.

Key Technologies

  • Open Charge Map API: Station metadata and geolocation
  • Mapbox Directions & Geocoding API: Route planning and spatial validation
  • NetworkX (Python): Network graph construction and centrality computation
  • Geopandas & Matplotlib: Geographic data analysis and visualization
  • Voronoi Diagrams, Clustering: Coverage assessment and accessibility gaps

Highlights

  • Identified high-traffic EV hubs via betweenness and degree centrality in San Francisco.
  • Pinpointed underserved charging deserts in regions like Bayview and Richmond.
  • Used shortest path analysis to detect routing bottlenecks and improve trip efficiency.
  • Generated buffer zones and Voronoi regions to assess coverage and service radii.
  • Clustered dense station areas (e.g., downtown SF) and suggested expansion areas.
